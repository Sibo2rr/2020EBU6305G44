The Wuhan battle was a battle between the Chinese army and the Japanese invaders in Wuhan during the Anti Japanese war. From June to October in 1938, the 5th and 9th theater troops of China launched along the north and south sides of the Yangtze River on the outskirts of Wuhan, covering the vast areas of Anhui, Henan, Jiangxi and Hubei provinces. It was the largest, longest and most annihilated campaign in the strategic defense phase of the anti Japanese war.
In this war, the Chinese army fought bloody battles hundreds of times, killing and injuring more than 257000 Japanese troops at the cost of more than 400000 casualties, which greatly consumed the living strength of the Japanese army. Although the Japanese army occupied Wuhan, its strategic plan of quick decision and forcing the national government to give in to end the war did not reach the goal. Since then, China's war of resistance against Japan entered a stage of strategic stalemate.

The Tunis campaign was an offensive campaign carried out by the British, American and French armies in the North Africa war of the Second World War from March 17 to May 13, 1943.
The purpose of the campaign was to smash the German Italian African Army Group and capture Tunisian territory, thus driving all German Italian allied forces out of North Africa. The Allied forces mainly include British forces, American forces and French forces.
At the beginning of the battle, the German army and the Italian army won the first victory, but the huge supply of supplies and the number of the dominant allied army ordered the axis army to lose completely. More than 230000 Italian and German soldiers became prisoners of war.

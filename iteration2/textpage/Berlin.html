The battle of Berlin (April 16 to May 9, 1945) was the last strategic offensive campaign carried out by the Soviet army in the Soviet German war of the Second World War.
The Soviet Army concentrated 270 divisions and cavalry divisions, 20 tank troops and mechanized troops, 14 air force groups, and a total of 2.5 million troops. It was also equipped with 2450 aircraft, 14200 guns, 1500 tanks and self-propelled guns. The enemy was the German group army group and the central group army group, with a total of 48 infantry divisions Nine motorized divisions, six armored divisions. A total of 800000 people, 10400 guns and mortars, 1500 tanks and assault guns, 3300 combat aircraft.
The campaign was launched on April 16, 1945, breaking through the defense lines of the Oder River and the nice river. Berlin was besieged on the 25th. During the attack on Berlin, the Soviet army took many ways to attack the center. After fierce street fighting, it broke into the central area of Berlin on April 27. On April 29, the Soviet army began to attack the parliament building. On the 30th, Hitler committed suicide in the basement of the prime minister's office. On May 2, general H. weidlin, commander of the garrison, led his troops to surrender, and the battle of Berlin ended.
On May 9, 1945, the representative of the German general headquarters, marshal W. Keitel, signed the unconditional surrender to the Soviet army and the Allied Expeditionary Forces in Berlin. During the whole campaign, the Soviet army captured 380000 German soldiers and seized more than 1500 tanks and self-propelled guns and 4500 planes.
The victory of this campaign smashed the German Supreme Command's plan to consume a lot of Soviet forces near Berlin and delay the war, marking the end of German Fascism and the end of European war.

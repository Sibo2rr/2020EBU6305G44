The battle of Okinawa was a battle between the U.S. and Japanese forces in Okinawa from March to July 1945. It was called "one of the most fierce and famous battles in the history of war" by Churchill.
The Okinawa campaign lasted more than three months, with 75000 casualties, 763 aircraft, 404 ships and 372 tanks lost; The Japanese army suffered 110000 casualties, 9000 people were captured, 7830 planes and 20 ships were lost. In addition, more than 100000 civilians died on the island.

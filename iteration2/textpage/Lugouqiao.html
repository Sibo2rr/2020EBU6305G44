The July 7 Incident, also known as the Lugouqiao Incident, took place on July 7, 1937.
On the night of July 7, 1937, the Japanese garrison in Lugouqiao, without informing the Chinese local authorities, held a so-called military exercise near the Chinese garrison position, and falsely claimed that a Japanese soldier was missing. They asked to enter the Wanping county (now Lugouqiao town) in the southwest of Beiping to search, but the Chinese garrison refused. The Japanese army immediately launched an attack on the Wanping City and Lugouqiao. The 219th regiment of the 37th division of the 29th army of the Chinese garrison fought back and resisted tenaciously.
The July 7th incident opened the prelude to the national anti Japanese war.

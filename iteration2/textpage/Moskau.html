The Moscow Defense War refers to a series of important strategic wars from September 1941 to January 1942 in the Soviet German war of the Second World War.
The war began in October 1941 and lasted until January 1942. The Moscow defense war included a series of defensive campaigns (September 30-December 5, 1941) and offensive campaigns (December 5, 1941-january 20, 1942) carried out by the Soviet army to smash the assault groups of the German "central" army group attacking Moscow.
The battle ended with the victory of the Soviet Red Army. The Soviet army won the battle of Moscow, which declared the end of Hitler's myth that "Blitzkrieg is invincible". It greatly inspired the determination of the world's anti fascist people to fight against aggression.

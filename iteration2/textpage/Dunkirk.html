The Dunkirk retreat was a military retreat of the British and French forces at the beginning of the Second World War.
On May 25, 1940, after the collapse of the British French defense line under the rapid attack of the German mechanized forces, the British army carried out the largest military retreat in the history of Dunkirk, a small port town in northwest France near the Belgian border.
Although this large-scale withdrawal operation, code named "generator plan", enabled Britain and France to withdraw a large number of troops by using various ships, successfully saved a large number of manpower, and preserved a living strength for the future counter offensive. However, all the heavy equipment of the British expeditionary forces stationed in France were abandoned on the European continent, which led to serious problems in the British ground defense.
The withdrawal marked the withdrawal of British forces from the European continent, and the main areas of Western Europe except Britain, Switzerland and Spain were occupied by Germany.

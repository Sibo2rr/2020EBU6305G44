The battle of Henan, Hunan and Guangxi was a large-scale offensive campaign carried out by the Japanese army throughout Henan, Hunan and Guangxi of China from April to December 1944.
The great retreat in the battle of Henan, Hunan and Guangxi is the second great retreat in the front battlefield of the Kuomintang since the Anti Japanese war. In eight months, China lost more than 500000-600000 troops in the battle of Henan, Hunan and Guangxi, lost four provincial capitals and 146 cities (38 cities lost in 37 days of the battle in central Henan), seven air bases and 36 airports, and lost more than 200000 square kilometers of territory and 60 million people.
At least 24000 Japanese soldiers died in the campaign, and 100000 people were injured and wounded.
Although the Japanese army reached a war attempt, it was unable to guarantee the smooth traffic of the mainland, and also failed to prevent the US aircraft from attacking Japan. In addition, the dispersion of troops provided conditions for the Kuomintang's counter offensive.

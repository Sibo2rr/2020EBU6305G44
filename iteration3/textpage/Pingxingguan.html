The great victory of Pingxingguan (also known as Pingxingguan battle and Pingxingguan ambush) refers to the first successful ambush of the Eighth Route Army in Pingxingguan on September 25, 1937, under the command of Lin Biao, commander of division 115 and Nie Rongzhen, deputy commander of division 115, in order to cooperate with the friendly forces in the second world war area and block the Japanese offensive, The Eighth Route Army won its first victory in Pingxingguan.
Lin Biao, the commander of the 115th division of the Eighth Route Army, led his headquarters to fight against the 21st brigade of the 5th division of the Japanese "steel army" in the face of danger according to the instructions of the Central Military Commission of the Communist Party of China. He won the first battle with the first brigade of the 21st brigade of the 5th division of the Japanese so-called "steel army" and the logistics convoy, which effectively cooperated with the defensive battle of the frontal battlefield of the second world war area in Yan Xishan's charge and delayed the Japanese strategic attack, It disrupted the enemy's plan to detour around North China on the right flank of the Ping Sui railway and was the first major victory of the Eighth Route Army since it left the army.

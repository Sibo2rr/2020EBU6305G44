Normandy landing: code named Operation Overlord, was a large-scale offensive launched by the allies on the western front of Europe in the Second World War.
Nearly three million soldiers crossed the English channel to Normandy, France.
The battle of Normandy is by far the largest sea landing operation in the world, which fundamentally changed the strategic situation of the Second World War.
Landing process: at 6:30 a.m. on June 6, 1944, a total of 176000 allied vanguard troops crossed the English Channel from England and landed in Normandy, capturing five beaches, namely Utah, Omaha, golden beach, Juno and Sword Beach;
Since then, 2.88 million allied troops have flooded into France and successfully opened up the second battlefield of the European continent.
It ended on August 19 after crossing the Seine man river.
